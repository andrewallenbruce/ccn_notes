1  Introduction

Published

December 25, 2025

A CMS Certification Number is a uniform way of identifying a healthcare provider or supplier that has or currently does participate in the Medicare and/or Medicaid programs.

The CCN is used to track provider agreements and cost reports. The National Provider Identifier (NPI) and Provider Transaction Account Number (PTAN) are tied to an entity’s CCN. The Regional Office (RO), not the State Agency (SA) or Medicare Administrative Contractor (MAC), assigns the CCN and maintains adequate controls.

One Term to Rule Them All

CCN replaced the terms Medicare Provider Number, Medicare Identification Number and OSCAR Number in 2007.

1.1 Canonical Format

A canonically valid CCN is an alphanumeric string, either 6 or 10 characters in length, that encodes a provider/supplier’s state and facility type.

1.2 States

A __CMS Certification Number__ is a uniform way of identifying a healthcare
provider or supplier that has or currently does participate in the Medicare and/or Medicaid programs.

The CCN is used to track provider agreements and cost reports. The National
Provider Identifier (NPI) and Provider Transaction Account Number (PTAN) are
tied to an entity's CCN. The Regional Office (RO), not the State Agency (SA) or Medicare Administrative Contractor (MAC), assigns the CCN and maintains adequate controls.

::: {#note-mod .callout-note}

### One Term to Rule Them All

__CCN__ replaced the terms _Medicare Provider Number_, _Medicare Identification Number_ 
and _OSCAR Number_ in [2007](https://www.cms.gov/medicare/provider-enrollment-and-certification/surveycertificationgeninfo/policy-and-memos-to-states-and-regions-items/cms1206256).

:::

## Canonical Format

A __*canonically valid* CCN__ is an alphanumeric string, either 6 or 10 characters in
length, that encodes a provider/supplier's state and facility type.
